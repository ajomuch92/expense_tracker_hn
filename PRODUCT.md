# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

android

## Users

Everyday personal users in Honduras tracking their own day-to-day spending in Lempira. They open the app on the go, often mid-errand, to log a purchase in a few seconds, then occasionally sit with it to see where the month's money went. They are not accountants or power-budgeters; the app has to work for anyone with a phone and a habit to build. It runs fully offline with no account.

## Product Purpose

A personal expense tracker that makes two things equally effortless: capturing an expense the instant it happens, and reading back a clear picture of spending by category and time period. Success is the user logging expenses without friction becoming a habit, and being able to answer "where did my money go this month?" at a glance. Everything is local and offline-first — no sign-up, no cloud dependency.

## Positioning

Effortless and offline. Log an expense in seconds and understand your spending, with zero account, zero setup, and no connection required.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, modern, efficient. The voice is direct and precise — no hand-holding, no cutesy filler. The interface should feel fast and confident, minimal chrome around the numbers that matter. Bilingual (Spanish-first, English supported).

## Anti-references

Must not feel like a cluttered spreadsheet: no dense tables, no tiny text, no dumping every number on screen at once. Information is filtered and summarized, not piled up.

## Design Principles

Capture-first: the fastest path is always adding an expense; that action is never more than one tap away.

Readable at a glance: each screen answers one question clearly. Summarize and filter by period/category rather than showing everything at once.

Offline by default: no state depends on a network. The app is instant and always available.

Precise over decorative: chrome stays out of the way of the numbers. Efficiency and clarity win over ornament.

Local relevance: Lempira currency and Spanish-first language are defaults, not afterthoughts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA: body text and numbers meet AA contrast against their backgrounds, type stays readable, tap targets are comfortably large, and motion respects the reduced-motion setting.
